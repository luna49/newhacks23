Circuit Fundamentals





Lesson Material

In our study of circuit analysis, three quantities that have the most relevance to us are voltage, current, and resistance.

The voltage between two points is defined as the energy required to move one unit of charge between the two points.
When solving a circuit, you may find it intuitive to imagine it as the amount of pressure that “pushes” electricity.
Remember that voltage has the unit of Volts (V), and always describes a difference in electric potential between two points.
